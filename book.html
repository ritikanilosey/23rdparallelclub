What to expect

Not just dinner. An evening you'll carry home.

You arrive to candlelight, the scent of spices, and eight seats around a single table. What follows is seven courses rooted in the kitchens of Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, and Gujarat — flavours most people have never tasted outside a grandmother's home. Between courses, a poem is read aloud in Hindi, its English meaning offered gently beside it. Strangers become conversationalists. Stories are shared. By the time dessert arrives — a Shahi Tukda infused with rare Mahua flower syrup — the table feels like it's always known each other. This is not a restaurant. It's an evening that changes how you think about food.
